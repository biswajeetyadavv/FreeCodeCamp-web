Vikram Batra

The Man who gave up his life to protect his Nation.

Indian Army officer known as "Sher Shah"

Here's a time line of Vikram Batra:

  • Full name: Captain Vikram Batra, PVC
  • Born: 9 September 1974 — Palampur (Bandla/Ghuggar), Himachal Pradesh, India.
  • Education: D.A.V. Public School (Palampur), Kendriya Vidyalaya (Palampur) and DAV College, Chandigarh.
  • Service: Commissioned into the Indian Army (13 JAK RIF). Service years 1997–1999.
  • Kargil War (1999): Led daring recapture operations including Point 5140 and the decisive capture of Point 4875 (where he was martyred).
  • Famous war-cry: Reportedly used the slogan “Yeh dil maange more” during the Kargil operations — became widely associated with him.
  • Award: Posthumously awarded the Param Vir Chakra (India’s highest military decoration for valour) on 15 August 1999.
  • Martyred: Killed in action on 7 July 1999 at Point 4875 (Kargil). He was 24 years old.
  • Personal: Fiancé — Dimple Cheema; remembered as brave, charismatic and highly motivating to his troops.
  • Legacy: Numerous memorials, buildings and institutions named after him; widely remembered in books, articles and films (e.g., the movie "Shershaah").
